The product

What exactly does Howard do?

Howard is an AI assistant built to execute tasks and expand its own capabilities over time. Out of the box it handles things like booking travel, managing your calendar, sending emails, and handling day-to-day administrative work — end to end, not just by suggestion. What makes Howard different is that it doesn't stop there. Howard builds new skills as you use it, learning what you need and developing the capabilities to do it. You interact with Howard through whatever messaging platform you prefer — configured during setup.

Is Howard a chatbot?

Technically, yes — Howard is built on large language models, so it can answer questions the same way ChatGPT can. The meaningful difference is what it can do beyond that. Howard has physical capabilities: it can take real action in the world, build and execute skills, and get things done end to end. When you ask Howard to book a flight, the flight gets booked. It's a chatbot in the same way a car is a skateboard — the category is technically accurate, but it misses the point.

What hardware does Howard run on?

Howard Core ships as a self-contained hardware unit with everything required for operation included in the box. It sits inside your home or office and processes by default on that hardware. You can optionally connect API keys to route non-sensitive queries through faster cloud models — but that's your choice to make, not a requirement.

How do I interact with Howard?

During setup, Howard walks you through connecting your preferred messaging platform. Most major messaging apps are supported. No new interface to learn — Howard meets you where you already communicate.

Does Howard work if my internet goes down?

Howard's core processing runs locally, so internet outages don't affect its ability to retain your preferences, history, and configuration. Some task execution — booking flights, sending emails — does require internet access to interact with external services. The assistant itself remains operational.

Privacy & data

Where does my data go?

By default, nowhere outside your building. Howard runs on hardware inside your home or office, and sensitive data never leaves it. If you choose to enable a cloud model for non-sensitive queries, Howard uses a separate classification agent to label every piece of data before any routing decision is made. Sensitive information is flagged and processed locally only. Non-sensitive information can be routed to a faster cloud model — but you configure what counts as sensitive, and the default is conservative.

Is my data used to train AI models?

Howard does not train the underlying AI models — your data never reaches a training server. What Howard does do is train itself: it learns your preferences, builds new skills it will need, and improves its own security based on issues it encounters. That learning stays on your hardware. The models stay unchanged. The only thing that gets smarter is Howard's understanding of you.

How is Howard different from cloud AI products on privacy?

Cloud AI products make privacy promises in policies. Howard makes the promise in architecture. Sensitive data never leaves your hardware — there's no server to receive it. If you opt into cloud processing for non-sensitive queries, a local classification agent makes that call before any data moves. You configure the rules. The default is to keep everything local.

What does the monthly subscription cover?

The subscription covers ongoing updates — primarily bug fixes and the maintenance of the runtime agents that keep Howard running correctly over time. It also includes security monitoring of your hardware and software stack, customer support, and a priority replacement warranty — meaning if your unit fails, a replacement ships the same day.
